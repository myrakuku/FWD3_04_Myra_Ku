Umi Jigoku

Tourist attraction in Beppu, Japan

The "Sea Hell"
(海地獄, Umi Jigoku)

Sea Hell is known for its vibrant blue-colored water. The water, which is 98 degrees Celsius, is used to boil onsen tamago (or "onsen eggs"). This is the starting point of the Jigoku Meguri tour. The cobalt-blue Sea Hell.

Best Way to Visit?

Beppu Station

take a 20-minute ride on a No. 2, 5, 24, or 41 bus and get off at Kannawa or Umi-Jigoku-mae stop

Buy a Meguri pass

The pass for all seven hells costs 2000 yen. Entry into hells individually is also possible for 400 yen per visit.

Seven hell highlights

The largest of the hells is Umi (Sea) Jigoku, so named because of its cobalt blue color. It is 200 meters deep. The boiling clay of Chinoike (blood lake) Jigoku is blood red; this is the oldest of Japan's hells.
